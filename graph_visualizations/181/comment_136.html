These graphs show total energy generation by country but the text says they exclude imports and exports so they probably don't represent usage by that country. If any of these countries are net importers of energy then their total consumption would be higher, whereas if they're net exporters it then it would be lower. We can figure out something about the trends in production though. Overall, energy production has nearly doubled in 22 years. Coal and gas still by far make up the largest part of the market however wind and solar production is increasing at the fastest rate. Nuclear and other seam to have slightly reduced in production and hydro slightly increased. There are two dips in production at around 2008 and 2019 (less pronounced in coal). I wonder what caused them. Something to do with the GFC and COVID19? In the US by comparison, coal production has decreased by more than half and oil has increased. Oil and gas combined has stayed fairly steady and still make up more than half of overall production. Overall US energy production has stayed fairly steady over the 22 years. India does not produce very much energy at all compared to the other countries and it is mostly coal.
